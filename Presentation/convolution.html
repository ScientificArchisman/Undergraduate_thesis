What is Convolution?

Convolution is a mathematical operation that is commonly used in signal processing and image processing to extract features from signals and images. The basic idea behind convolution is to slide a small matrix, called a kernel, over a larger matrix, called an input matrix, and calculate a dot product between the kernel and the portion of the input matrix that the kernel is currently overlaid on.

Padding and Strides

Padding refers to the addition of extra pixels around the border of an image, which allows us to preserve the spatial dimensions of the input image when we apply convolutional filters. This is important because convolutional filters tend to reduce the spatial dimensions of an image, which can lead to loss of information. Padding can also help to reduce the amount of information lost at the edges of an image, which can be particularly important for objects or features that are located near the edges.

Strides, on the other hand, refer to the step size that we use when we slide the convolutional filters over the input image. A larger stride will result in a smaller output size, while a smaller stride will result in a larger output size. Strides are important because they can affect the receptive field of the convolutional filters, which in turn can affect the ability of the model to capture different features at different scales. Choosing the appropriate stride is an important consideration when designing a convolutional neural network.
